## Detailed Reviews

### Aviva Labs

Aviva Labs has become the gold standard for professional mobile spray tan artists in the U.S. Their solutions use beet sugar-derived DHA, which produces a warmer, more natural tone with minimal "spray tan smell." The product line includes multiple shade levels and undertone options, giving artists precise control over the final color.

What sets Aviva apart is the fade. Clients consistently report that Aviva-based tans fade evenly over 8–12 days rather than becoming patchy. The solution dries quickly, which is a practical advantage for mobile artists working in clients' homes.

**Best for**: Professional mobile artists who want the highest-quality result and are willing to pay premium product costs.

### Norvell

Norvell is one of the most widely used professional spray tan solutions in the United States. Their product range is extensive — from ultra-dark competition formulas to light, natural-looking everyday shades. The Venetian line is particularly popular among artists for its warm, golden-brown tones.

Norvell solutions work well in both HVLP spray guns and airbrush systems. They are reliable, well-formulated, and widely available through professional beauty supply channels.

**Best for**: Professional artists who need a versatile, proven solution that works across a wide range of client skin tones.

### SunFX

SunFX is an Australian-made solution that has built a strong following among artists who prioritize clean ingredients. It is ECOCERT-certified organic, vegan, cruelty-free, and free from parabens, alcohol, and artificial fragrances. Despite the clean formulation, SunFX delivers excellent color depth and longevity.

The solution is lightweight and dries quickly without a sticky residue. Artists working with clients who have sensitive skin, eczema, or allergies often recommend SunFX specifically because it causes fewer reactions than competitors.

**Best for**: Clients with sensitive skin and artists who want an organic, eco-certified option without sacrificing performance.

### St. Tropez Professional

St. Tropez is the most recognized name in sunless tanning worldwide, and their professional line lives up to the reputation. The solutions produce a natural, warm brown that photographs beautifully, making St. Tropez a popular choice for bridal and editorial work.

The professional line differs significantly from the consumer self-tanning products you see at Sephora. It is formulated for HVLP spray application and develops more evenly than the consumer mousses and mists.

One drawback: St. Tropez professional solutions tend to have a longer drying time than competitors like Aviva or SunFX. Artists need to allow extra dry time before clients can dress.

**Best for**: Bridal work, editorial shoots, and luxury salon environments where the brand name carries weight with clients.

### Bondi Sands Professional

Bondi Sands built its reputation in Australia and has expanded aggressively into the U.S. professional market. Their professional solutions are more affordable than Aviva or St. Tropez, making them a popular choice for artists who want to keep session prices competitive.

The color profile leans warm and golden — true to the "Australian beach" aesthetic. Results are natural-looking, though not as customizable as Aviva or Norvell since the shade range is narrower.

**Best for**: Artists who want a solid, affordable professional solution with warm, natural-looking results.

### Loving Tan Professional

Loving Tan is known for delivering deep, dark, dramatic color. Their professional solutions are popular among artists whose clients want a bold, "just came back from Bali" look rather than a subtle sun-kissed glow.

The formulas include a heavy cosmetic bronzer that gives immediate color after application, which clients love because they see results right away. However, that bronzer can transfer to clothing and bedsheets more than lighter formulas.

**Best for**: Clients who want deep, dramatic color and do not mind a heavier cosmetic bronzer.

### Tan-Luxe

Tan-Luxe takes a different approach. Rather than a single-application spray tan, their products are designed for gradual, buildable color. The Super Glow line uses hyaluronic acid and plant-derived DHA for a skincare-meets-tanning approach.

Professional artists primarily use Tan-Luxe for facial tanning drops and as a maintenance product between full-body sessions. It is not a replacement for a professional spray tan, but it is an excellent complement.

**Best for**: Facial tanning, gradual color building, and maintenance between professional sessions.

### Dolce Glow

Dolce Glow entered the market with a clean beauty positioning and has quickly built a loyal following. Their solutions are vegan, cruelty-free, and formulated without parabens, sulfates, or phthalates. The DHA is derived from sugar, and the formulas include skin-nourishing ingredients like vitamin E and aloe.

Color results lean natural and golden. The brand is popular in the celebrity and influencer spray tan circuit, which has driven awareness among consumers who specifically request it.

**Best for**: Clean beauty-conscious clients and artists who want a premium, Instagram-friendly brand in their kit.

### Mine Tan

Mine Tan (by MineTan) specializes in rapid-develop formulas. Their Express line develops in as little as one hour, compared to the 6–10 hours most solutions require. This makes Mine Tan a practical choice for artists working with clients who have time constraints — same-day event tans, for example.

The tradeoff is that rapid-develop formulas tend to fade slightly less evenly than standard-develop solutions. For clients who need a tan fast, that tradeoff is usually worth it.

**Best for**: Express spray tans, same-day event prep, and clients who cannot commit to an 8-hour development window.

## What to Ask Your Spray Tan Artist

When booking a spray tan, do not hesitate to ask your artist which solution they use and why. A knowledgeable artist will be happy to explain their product choice. Here are good questions to ask:

- **What brand of solution do you use?** Any reputable artist will answer this immediately.
- **Is the formula vegan/organic?** If this matters to you, ask upfront.
- **Do you offer different shade levels?** Good artists stock at least 2–3 depth options.
- **What is the DHA percentage?** Lower percentages (6–8%) produce lighter results; higher percentages (10–14%) produce deeper color.
- **Do you offer rapid-develop options?** Important if you have a tight timeline.

## The Bottom Line

The best spray tan solution depends on your priorities. For the highest overall quality, Aviva Labs and SunFX lead the pack. For value, Bondi Sands and Norvell deliver professional results at a lower cost. For clean beauty, Dolce Glow and SunFX are the top choices. For bridal work, St. Tropez Professional remains the industry standard.

Whatever your preference, the most important thing is that your artist uses a professional-grade solution — not a consumer product repackaged for spray gun application. A good solution, applied by a skilled artist, is the recipe for a flawless glow.
