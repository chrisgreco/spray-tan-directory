A mobile spray tan costs **$55 to $150** depending on your city, the artist's experience level, and the quality of the tanning solution used. Most clients in mid-size U.S. cities pay between $65 and $95 for a standard full-body mobile spray tan session. Premium artists in major metros like Miami, Los Angeles, and New York often charge $100 or more, especially for bridal or event-specific appointments.

Understanding what drives spray tan pricing helps you budget for your glow and avoid overpaying — or worse, underpaying for a subpar result. Here is everything you need to know about spray tan costs in 2026.

## Spray Tan Cost by City Tier

Where you live plays a major role in pricing. Artists in higher cost-of-living cities charge more to cover their expenses, and demand in sun-conscious markets tends to push prices up further.

| City Tier | Example Cities | Typical Range |
|---|---|---|
| Major Metro (High COL) | New York, Los Angeles, Miami, San Francisco | $85–$150 |
| Major Metro (Moderate COL) | Nashville, Denver, Austin, Atlanta | $65–$110 |
| Mid-Size City | Tampa, Phoenix, Dallas, Portland | $55–$95 |
| Small City / Suburban | Boise, Knoxville, Tulsa, Mesa | $45–$80 |

Keep in mind that these ranges reflect mobile spray tan pricing. Salon booth tans are typically 30–50% less expensive but offer far less customization and personal attention.

## What Factors Affect Spray Tan Pricing?

### Solution Quality

The tanning solution is the single most important factor in your result, and it is also a significant cost driver for the artist. Professional-grade solutions from brands like Aviva Labs, Norvell, and SunFX cost artists $8–$20 per application in product alone. Organic and vegan formulations tend to sit at the higher end. Artists using premium solutions charge more, but clients consistently report better color, smoother fading, and fewer reactions.

### Artist Experience and Certifications

A spray tan artist with five years of experience, a strong portfolio, and professional certifications (such as through the Sunless Association) will typically charge 20–40% more than someone just starting out. That premium reflects their ability to color-match accurately, contour effectively, and troubleshoot skin types that are difficult to tan evenly.

### Travel Distance

Mobile spray tan artists factor travel into their pricing. Most include travel within a 10–15 mile radius in their base price. Beyond that, expect a $10–$25 travel surcharge. Some artists in sprawling metros like Los Angeles or Dallas have tiered travel fees based on zone.

### Appointment Timing

Weekend and evening appointments often carry a $10–$20 premium, especially during peak seasons like prom (April–June) and wedding season (May–October). Last-minute bookings within 24 hours may also incur a rush fee.

### Add-On Services

Many artists offer add-ons that increase the total cost:

- **Rapid rinse formula**: +$10–$20
- **Bronzing drops or contouring**: +$15–$25
- **Barrier cream application**: Often included, sometimes +$5
- **Exfoliation mitt or prep kit**: +$10–$15
- **Touch-up session (next day)**: +$25–$40

## How to Get the Best Value on a Spray Tan

### Book a Package or Membership

Many mobile spray tan artists offer package deals for repeat clients. A common structure is 3 sessions for $180–$250 (saving $15–$30 per session) or a monthly membership at $70–$90 per visit. If you tan regularly, this is by far the most cost-effective approach.

### Book With a Bridal Party or Group

Group bookings of three or more people usually qualify for per-person discounts of 10–20%. This is especially popular for bridal parties, bachelorette weekends, and prom groups.

### Ask About New Client Specials

Many artists offer a first-time client rate that is $10–$20 below their standard price. This lets them build their client base while giving you a chance to test their work at a lower risk.

### Avoid the Cheapest Option

A spray tan under $40 from a mobile artist is a red flag. At that price point, the artist is almost certainly using a low-quality solution, cutting corners on prep, or both. Patchy results, orange tones, and chemical smells are far more common with bargain spray tans. It is worth paying $65–$95 for a result you are actually happy with.

## Is a Spray Tan Worth the Cost?

For most clients, absolutely. A professional spray tan delivers a natural-looking glow in under 30 minutes with zero UV exposure. Compared to a tanning bed membership ($30–$80/month with documented skin cancer risk) or a self-tanner that takes 45 minutes to apply and often streaks, a professional spray tan offers the best balance of convenience, safety, and results.

When you factor in the confidence boost for a wedding, vacation, or special event, the $65–$95 most people spend on a mobile spray tan delivers exceptional value per wear. A well-maintained tan lasts 7–10 days, bringing the daily cost to under $10 for that sun-kissed look.

## Bottom Line

The average spray tan costs between $55 and $150, with most clients paying $65–$95 for a quality mobile session. Your exact price depends on where you live, the type of service you book, and the artist's experience level. Focus on finding a well-reviewed artist who uses professional-grade solutions, and you will be happy with both the result and the investment.
