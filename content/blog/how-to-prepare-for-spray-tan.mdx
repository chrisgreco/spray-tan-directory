Proper preparation is the difference between a spray tan that looks flawless for 10 days and one that fades unevenly after three. The good news is that prep is simple — it just requires planning ahead. Follow this complete checklist in the 24 hours before your appointment and you will walk away with the best possible result.

## The Complete Pre-Tan Checklist

### 1. Exfoliate 12–24 Hours Before Your Appointment

Exfoliation is the single most important prep step. It removes dead skin cells that would otherwise absorb DHA unevenly, causing dark patches, streaks, and premature fading.

Use a physical exfoliator (a scrub or exfoliating mitt) rather than a chemical one. Chemical exfoliants containing AHAs, BHAs, or glycolic acid can create a barrier on your skin that interferes with DHA absorption. An oil-free body scrub or a simple exfoliating glove with body wash works perfectly.

**Pay extra attention to these areas:**
- Elbows and knees (thick skin absorbs more DHA)
- Ankles and feet (same issue)
- Wrists and hands
- Neck and jawline
- Any areas with dry skin buildup

Do not exfoliate the morning of your appointment — you want your skin to calm down and your pores to close before the solution is applied.

### 2. Shave or Wax at the Right Time

**Shaving**: Shave at least 12 hours before your spray tan, ideally the night before. Freshly shaved skin has open pores that trap DHA, creating a "strawberry legs" effect — tiny dark dots at each follicle. Giving your skin 12+ hours to settle prevents this.

**Waxing**: Wax at least 48 hours before your spray tan. Waxing removes the top layer of skin along with the hair, and your skin needs time to heal and normalize before DHA application. Waxing too close to your appointment almost always results in irritation and uneven color.

**Laser hair removal**: Wait at least one week after a laser session before spray tanning.

### 3. Skip the Moisturizer, Deodorant, and Makeup

On the day of your spray tan, do not apply anything to your skin after your shower:

- **No body lotion or moisturizer** — Creates a barrier that prevents DHA absorption
- **No deodorant** — The aluminum compounds react with DHA and create green or gray patches in your underarms
- **No perfume or body spray** — Alcohol-based products interfere with even absorption
- **No makeup or foundation** — Blocks the solution from reaching your face
- **No body oils or serums** — Oil repels the water-based tanning solution

Your skin should be completely clean, dry, and product-free when your artist arrives.

### 4. Remove All Jewelry

Take off rings, bracelets, necklaces, anklets, watches, and any body jewelry before your appointment. Jewelry creates tan lines and blocks the solution from reaching the skin underneath. Even a thin ring will leave a noticeable white line.

If you wear jewelry you cannot remove (a wedding band you never take off, for example), let your artist know so they can work around it.

### 5. Get a Manicure and Pedicure Beforehand

If you have nail appointments planned, schedule them before your spray tan, not after. The soaking, scrubbing, and chemicals involved in a mani-pedi will strip the tan from your hands and feet. Gel and acrylic nails are fine to have done before your tan — just make sure any cuticle oil is thoroughly washed off.

### 6. Wear the Right Clothing

What you wear to and after your appointment matters more than you might think.

**Wear to your appointment:**
- Loose, dark clothing (the solution can transfer to fabric during development)
- A flowy dress or oversized T-shirt and loose shorts work well
- Flip-flops or slides (no socks or tight shoes)
- A dark, loose sports bra or go braless if comfortable

**Avoid:**
- Tight jeans, leggings, or skinny jeans
- White or light-colored clothing
- Tight bras with underwire (creates lines)
- Socks, boots, or closed-toe shoes
- Anything with elastic bands that press into the skin

### 7. Handle Skin Concerns in Advance

**Dry patches**: If you have eczema-prone areas or chronically dry skin, moisturize those spots well in the days leading up to your appointment (but not the day of). Well-hydrated skin absorbs DHA more evenly.

**Cuts or scrapes**: Fresh wounds will absorb DHA aggressively and turn much darker than surrounding skin. If you have a healing cut, let your artist know so they can apply barrier cream over it.

**Sunburn**: Do not get a spray tan over sunburned skin. The burn is actively peeling and your tan will be extremely uneven. Wait until the burn has fully healed.

**Self-tanner residue**: If you have leftover self-tanner on your skin, exfoliate it off completely before your spray tan. Layering professional DHA over patchy self-tanner creates an uneven result.

### 8. Plan Your Post-Tan Schedule

Before your appointment, think about what comes after:

- **Do not work out or sweat** for at least 8 hours (or until your first rinse). Sweat streaks through developing DHA.
- **Do not shower** for the time your artist recommends (usually 6–10 hours for standard solutions, 1–3 hours for rapid/express formulas).
- **Do not swim** for at least 24 hours.
- **Avoid rain** — if it is raining on your appointment day, have an umbrella ready.

Planning your first rinse timing is important. If your appointment is at 6 PM with a standard solution, you will rinse at around midnight or the next morning. If that timing does not work for you, ask about a rapid-develop formula that lets you rinse in 1–3 hours.

## Day-Of Timeline

Here is a simple timeline for the day of your spray tan:

**Morning (if afternoon appointment):**
- Shower and exfoliate lightly (if you did not exfoliate the night before)
- Shave if needed (though the night before is better)
- Do not apply any products to your skin

**30 minutes before appointment:**
- Remove all jewelry
- Put on loose, dark clothing and flip-flops
- Make sure the space is clear if you booked a mobile artist (they need roughly a 6x6 foot area)

**During appointment (15–25 minutes):**
- Your artist will apply barrier cream to hands, feet, elbows, and knees
- Stand as directed and breathe normally
- The appointment itself is quick

**After appointment:**
- Air-dry for 5–10 minutes before getting dressed
- Wear your loose, dark clothing home (or stay home if you booked mobile)
- Do not sit on light-colored furniture without a dark towel underneath
- Wait the recommended time before your first rinse

## Quick-Reference Prep Checklist

- [ ] Exfoliate 12–24 hours before
- [ ] Shave the night before (or 12+ hours before)
- [ ] Wax at least 48 hours before
- [ ] No moisturizer, deodorant, or makeup on tan day
- [ ] Remove all jewelry
- [ ] Mani/pedi done before your tan appointment
- [ ] Loose, dark clothing and flip-flops ready
- [ ] Post-tan schedule cleared (no gym, pool, or shower for 6–10 hours)

Follow these steps and your spray tan artist can deliver the absolute best result. Preparation accounts for at least half of how good your final tan looks — the rest is up to your artist's skill and the quality of the solution. Nail the prep, and you are setting yourself up for a gorgeous, even, long-lasting glow.
